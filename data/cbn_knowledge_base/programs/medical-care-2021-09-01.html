International Medical Care Provided Through the Following Methods:


Medical and Dental Brigades

In areas where healthcare isn't readily available or affordable, OB provides physical care through medical and dental brigades, which entails medical professionals working alongside communities to establish sustainable health systems. Medical and dental professionals treat sickness, injuries and dental problems, distribute much-need medication and offer referrals. Through these brigades, you are meeting desperate needs in rural communities. 

Life-Changing Surgeries

Providing life-changing surgery is an essential part of the international medical relief outreach you make possible. Whether it's surgery for cleft lips and palates, clubfoot, orthopedic issues, eyesight, burns, or even repairing a hole in a child's heart, you are changing and even saving, lives. In partnership with surgeons, hospitals, government agencies, and caring people, we're able to provide those who are living in poverty with the operations they need. 

Here are some of the countries our partners have touched with the gift of surgery worldwide.

  • Ukraine
  • India
  • China
  • Honduras
  • Haiti
  • Kenya
  • Guatemala

Click here to view the Life-Changing Surgeries Catalog to view both funded and pending surgery initiatives.

Medical Distribution

Through medical and "gifts-in-kind" that OB receives via corporations donating medicines, vitamins, medical supplies and more, we are able to offer much-needed medication and supplies to clinics and hospitals in poor areas, allowing them to give quality care to patients and much-needed medicine at no cost.

Disease Prevention

Operation Blessing makes possible classes in hygiene, health, dental care, and nutrition around the world through the following methods:

  • Sending healthy meals to school children in need 
  • Offering vital health screenings, like ultrasounds and cervical cancer testing, as well as screenings for issues like diabetes and high blood pressure 
  • Providing preventative community programs to combat mosquito-borne illnesses such as chikungunya, dengue, malaria, and zika in places like Honduras and Kenya

Community Health Volunteers

Your support allows Operation Blessing to train Community Health Volunteers to serve as front-line health promoters in remote villages where medical care is hard to reach, bringing vital help and information to those in need. The Community Health Volunteers are trained to provide the following:

  • Life-saving basic care and health education
  • First-aid kits and other medical supplies to share with the community
