Clean Water Provided Internationally Through the Following Methods:

Community Water Systems

In areas like Honduras, you make it possible to capture spring water, build water storage tanks and pipe networks that bring clean drinking water right into the homes of poor villages.  In places like Mexico, OB provides rainwater harvesting systems as well as chlorination to ensure water is safe to drink.  This transforms the lives of people who previously suffered from the water crisis and had to get their water from dirty ponds and streams.

Desalination

After Hurricane Maria devastated Puerto Rico, your faithful support and a partnership with Parker Hannifin, one of the world's largest manufacturers of water processing equipment, allowed Operation Blessing to test and develop water desalination systems.  This provided safe drinking water to survivors surrounded by saltwater.

Chlorination

Operation Blessing uses a variety of chlorine generators to produce chlorine using just common salt and water. This chlorine can be used to disinfect water for needy communities and in times disaster.  This chlorine also provides disinfection for hospitals in Africa, Haiti and Latin America.

Water Filtration

You are helping provide water filtration units to families and villages where the water just needs to be filtered in order to be safe or as temporary solution while water systems are being built and in times of disaster.

Rain Catchment Systems

Operation Blessing can provide individual water catchment systems for each family in need in communities without clean water, These systems can include gutters for collecting the water, leaf filters, water storage tanks, chlorine dispensers and water purifiers and faucets.

Hand Pump Wells

In places like Asia and Africa, your love and kindness allows Operation Blessing to install hand pump wells that supply clean water to whole villages.

Drilling Water Wells 

You are helping to dig deep, solar-powered water wells in places likes Kenya, where the abundant sun and frequent drought create a water shortage.
