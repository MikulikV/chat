  • "Lord Jesus… I ask you to come into my life… I want to turn… from living my life… under my own control… Come now… and live your life in me… Cleanse me from my sins… I receive you as my Lord… and my Savior… I will live for you… and serve you… all the days of my life… In Jesus' name... Amen."

  • "Heavenly Father, my heart rejoices for (caller name), who has just become your child. Pour your richest blessing into (caller name)'s life. Help (him/her) to grow in this new relationship with you as (he/she) prays and studies your Word. Fill (caller name) with the power and the presence of your Holy Spirit. In Jesus' name... Amen."

Why Salvation?

The following information can be used to share why salvation is needed.

All are Separated from God

"Everyone who tries to live life his own way is separated from God."

Scripture References:

  • For all have sinned; all fall short of God's glorious standard. (Romans 3:23) 
  • All of us have strayed away like sheep. We have left God's paths to follow our own. Yet the LORD laid on him the guilt and sins of us all."  (Isaiah 53:6)
 

The Penalty for Separation

"We will be held accountable for the choices we make.  If we choose to ignore God, we'll face eternal separation from Him."

Scripture References: 

  • For the wages of sin is death, but the free gift of God is eternal life through Christ Jesus our Lord. (Romans 6:23) 
  • And just as it is destined that each person dies only once and after that comes judgment. (Hebrews 9:27a)
 

Good Works are not Enough

"Nothing we do, no matter how good it may be, can make us good enough to have a relationship with God without Jesus Christ."

Scripture References: 

  • He saved us, not because of the good things we did, but because of his mercy. He washed away our sins and gave us a new life through the Holy Spirit. (Titus 3:5) 
  • God saved you by his special favor when you believed.  And you can't take credit for this; it is a gift from God. Salvation is not a reward for the good things we have done, so none of us can boast about it. (Ephesians 2:8-9)
 

Jesus Paid the Penalty

"God sent Jesus to be the bridge back to right-standing with God.  Christ's death on the cross paid the penalty we faced for going our own way."

Scripture References:  

  • But God showed his great love for us by sending Christ to die for us while we were still sinners. (Romans 5:8) 
  • Christ also suffered when he died for our sins once for all time. He never sinned, but he died for sinners that he might bring us safely home to God. He suffered physical death, but he was raised to life in the Spirit. (1 Peter 3:18)
 

Must Receive Christ

"You must receive God's gift by inviting Jesus into your life as your Lord and Savior."

Scripture References:   

  • But to all who believed him and accepted him, he gave the right to become children of God. (John 1:12) 
  • Look!  Here I stand at the door and knock. If you hear me calling and open the door, I will come in, and we will share a meal as friends. (Revelation 3:20) 
 

Assurance of Salvation

"Once you have done that, you can have absolute assurance that you have eternal life."

Scripture References:    

  • I write this to you who believe in the Son of God, so that you may know you have eternal life. (1 John 5:13) 
  • I assure you, those who listen to my message and believe in God who sent me have eternal life. They will never be condemned for their sins, but they have already passed from death into life.  (John 5:24)
 

 

 

Encouragement 

The following information can be used to offer encouragement to the new or recommitted believer. 

God Strengthens & Guides

"God promises to strengthen and guide you now that you are His child." 

Scripture References:

  • "Don't be afraid, for I am with you. Do not be dismayed, for I am your God. I will strengthen you. I will help you. I will uphold you with my victorious right hand. (Isaiah 41:10) 
  • "Trust in the LORD with all your heart; do not depend on your own understanding. Seek his will in all you do, and he will direct your paths. (Proverbs 3:5-6)
 

Sin Forgiven

"In God's eye you are now a new creation. All your sins have been forgiven and you have eternal life."

Scripture References:

  • "Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation; old things have passed away; behold, all things have become new." (2 Corinthians 5:17)
  • "I assure you, those who listen to my message and believe in God who sent me have eternal life. They will never be condemned for their sins, but they have already passed from death into life." (John 5:24)
  • "But if we confess our sins to him, he is faithful and just to forgive us and to cleanse us from everything wrong." (1 John 1:9)
 

Heir of God's Kingdom

"You are now an heir in God's Kingdom.  He will watch over you and provide for you."

Scripture References:

  • Now you are no longer a slave but God's own child. And since you are his child, everything he has belongs to you. (Galatians 4:7)
  • And this same God who takes care of me will supply all your needs from his glorious riches, which have been given to us in Christ Jesus. (Philippians 4:19)
 

God Answers Prayer

"Now that you are His child, God welcomes your prayers.  He promises to listen and respond when you call on Him."

Scripture References:

  • Morning, noon, and night I plead aloud in my distress, and the LORD hears my voice. (Psalm 55:17) 
  • I will answer them before they even call to me. While they are still talking to me about their needs, I will go ahead and answer their prayers! (Isaiah 65:24) 
  • You haven't done this before. Ask, using my name, and you will receive, and you will have abundant joy. (John 16:24)

The Prayer of the Baptism of the Holy Spirit 

(Have caller repeat after you) 

  • "Lord Jesus… I ask you now… to pour out your Holy Spirit on me… Fill me to overflowing… with Your Spirit… I surrender everything within me… to your control… and receive your gifts by faith… I receive from you a prayer language… so that I can praise you… in a new way… Thank you for giving me this gift… Help me to release myself… so that you might flow through me… In Jesus' name... Amen."

Why Baptism of the Holy Spirit?

The following information can be used to share the baptism of the holy spirit. 

Power to Witness

"The outpouring of the Holy Spirit will enable you to be a more effective witness for Jesus.  The Holy Spirit empowers us to speak out with boldness about our relationship to Jesus."

Scripture References:

  • But when the Holy Spirit has come upon you, you will receive power and will tell people about me everywhere - in Jerusalem, throughout Judea, in Samaria, and to the ends of the earth. (Acts 1:8)
 

Special Gift

"The outpouring of the Holy Spirit is a special gift from God through which He fills your inner being with His presence. It is available to every born-again believer."

Scripture References:

  • Peter replied, "Each of you must turn from your sins and turn to God, and be baptized in the name of Jesus Christ for the forgiveness of your sins. Then you will receive the gift of the Holy Spirit. This promise is to you and to your children, and even to the Gentiles - all who have been called by the Lord our God." (Acts 2:38-39.)
 

Received by Faith

"Just as you received salvation by faith, you also receive outpouring of the Holy Spirit the very moment you ask in faith."

Scripture References:

  • If you sinful people know how to give good gifts to your children, how much more will your heavenly Father give the Holy Spirit to those who ask Him. (Luke 11:13)
 

Prayer Language and Other Gifts

"When you receive the outpouring of the Holy Spirit, God wants to give you a special language with which you can praise and worship Him unhindered by our understanding.  Sometimes the Holy Spirit will manifest Himself in other ways, such as prophecy, overflowing joy and other gifts which clearly reveal the presence and reality of Jesus Christ."

Scripture References:  

  • For if I pray in tongues, my spirit is praying, but I don't understand what I am saying. Well then, what shall I do? I will do both. I will pray in the spirit, and I will pray in words I understand. I will sing in the spirit, and I will sing in words I understand. (1 Corinthians 14:14-15) 
  • And the Holy Spirit helps us in our distress. For we don't even know what we should pray for, nor how we should pray. But the Holy Spirit prays for us with groanings that cannot be expressed in words. (Romans 8:26)

Agent Note: PSR Note: If Prayer Language is not evident...remind the caller that they have received the Baptism of the Holy Spirit by faith. If there is no immediate manifestation of tongues, that does not mean they have not been filled with the Holy Spirit.
