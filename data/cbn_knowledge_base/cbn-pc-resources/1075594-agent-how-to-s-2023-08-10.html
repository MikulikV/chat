The G.E.N.E.S.I.S Model

 The G.E.N.E.S.I.S Model is the foundation of every great call. Click on the accordions below to learn more about each key stage of a call.

G.E.N.E.S.I.S Standard Definitions

Energized Displaying a sense of vitality and enthusiasm.
Friendly Modeling kindness and a pleasant demeanor.
Warm Having showing, or expressing enthusiasm, affection, or kindness. 
Responsive Listening Modeling a sense of engagement while listening to another by smiling, displaying an appropriate facial expression and tone of voice, minimizing distractions to ensure full comprehension of what is being shared, and reaffirming what you hear the caller sharing. 
Gratitude The quality of being thankful; readiness to show appreciation for and to return kindness.
Empathy The ability to understand and share the feeling of another; building a sense of connection in the process. 
Ownership The act of taking responsibility in our words and actions, operating with confident initiative as a representative of CBN, with full accountability for the quality of the caller's experience. 
Professionalism Demonstrating a commitment to giving an elevated exemplary honor towards every caller in our words and actions ex. of ways to display professionalism in our actions: ownership, honesty, dependability, humility, and politeness. 
Solution-Driven Attitude Demonstrating a commitment to giving an elevated experience by displaying an attitude centered in resolving conflict and overcoming obstacles to achieve positive results; asking yourself "what else can I do to achieve results?"
Statement of Faith A brief statement that aligns with the truth of Scripture that is shared to strengthen the faith of the caller (ex. we are believing for your healing! Or, we know God has heard this prayer and can trust Him!)
Humility Submission to Christ first and foremost in our words and actions, putting others before ourselves just as Christ modeled to us.

Greet

Using an energized, friendly, and warm tone, greet each caller as if they were being welcomed into your home.

Ways to Ensure a Great Greeting

  1. Facial Expression:  Act as if others can see you! When you smile it naturally changes the tone and pitch of your voice to sound more pleasant! 
  2. Connection to the Vine: Jesus is clear in John 15:5 when He shares that apart from Him, we can do nothing. If we want to love our callers in the first few seconds of the call, it is vital we connect to the vine (Jesus) and allow Him to bear good fruit in us!
  3. Voice Inflection: Just as singers enhance their singing voice by singing from their diaphragm, we as Agents, can change the richness of our speaking voice by speaking from our diaphragm. 
  4. Remember the 2-second Rule: The first impression you give at the Greeting phase will frame the caller’s perception of you and CBN for the rest of the call.

Speech Clarity

 There are three main components that ensure speech clarity: 

  1. Speak plainly
  2. Articulate your words with precise consonants and vowels
  3. Your communication should be meaningful, communicating the truth, and providing the caller with answers

Some habits that can be harmful to speech clarity are: 

  1. Blurring words 
  2. Using slang words 
  3. Using CBN jargon
  4. Speaking too softly

Inspiring with your Voice

Learn 4 key tips to improve your tone by voice artist and voice tone consultant, Janina Heron:

  1. Breath – Learning to control your breathing is key to voice tone.  It is also the key to stamina, energy, and power. Practice using your diaphragm to give you a strong, confident tone. 
  2. Warm Vocal Tone – A warm vocal tone is a sound that embodies humor, empathy, compassion, and friendliness.  It is the sound of welcome, acceptance, and love.  Smiling brings warmth to your voice!  Warmth brings connection with your caller! 
  3. Passionate Vocal Tone – A passionate vocal tone is speaking with expression, energy, and variety.  Passion is the pacing of your words whether fast or slow and voice inflection. 
  4. Confident Vocal Tone – Confidence is expressed with a measured pace; don’t rush through your words as you engage. 


*Tips shared from Janina's Ted Talks YouTube video "Inspiring with Your Voice".

Engage

Continue to maintain an energized, friendly, and warm vocal tone as you practice responsive listening techniques, constantly displaying a spirit of empathy and gratitude.  

Ways to Ensure Great Engagement

1. Responsive Listening - Let the caller know that you are tracking with them and are willing to hear more. 

Tips to Demonstrate Responsive Listening: 
  • Tune Out Distractions 
  • Take Notes
  • Listen to Respond, not to React 
  • Responsive Gestures  
  • Acknowledge and Confirm 

2. Connect to the Vine - (John 15:1-8)        
3. Act Out Empathy and Gratitude - When a person calls for prayer or has a story or need that is difficult, we should model empathy to build connection. When a person calls for a fun reason, such as to share a praise report, become a partner, order a product, and even for a customer service issue, we should energize them with gratitude! 

4 Components of True Empathy:
  • Perspective Talking - The first step is taking the time to acknowledge that someone’s perspective is their truth – even if your perception or perspective is different from theirs. 
  • Staying out of Judgement -  Part of modeling empathy is remembering to stay out of judgment as they share their perspective of their truth. 
  • Recognizing Emotion in Others -  True empathy is not about relating to the circumstance or event, but to the feelings associated with it. Finding a way to relate to the emotions of another is a key fundamental to modeling true empathy. 
  • Communicate Emotion - You may not have an easy answer or a quick fix but taking a moment to stand alongside someone is often the best way to show compassion.


4. Personalize with their Name - Honor the caller by asking for their name & use their name throughout the call experience.

L.A.U.R.A - Building the Perfect Empathy Statement

L.A.U.R.A is an acronym that teaches us the steps, or “ingredients” that make a “sweet” statement of empathy. 


L is for Listen – Just as flour is the foundation to a great cake, listening is the foundation to building a great empathetic connection. 


A is for Acknowledge – Just as the sugar sweetens the cake batter, acknowledging what we specifically heard sweetens our sense of empathy towards the caller. 


U is for Understand – Just as eggs bind together ingredients in a recipe, understanding the caller binds our connection as we empathize with them. 


R is for Relate – Just as butter makes the batter richer and more flavorful, relating to the caller’s experience makes our empathy richer. 


A is for Act – Just as a splash of vanilla or a scoop of chocolate makes the cake commit to a flavor, Act is the final step in empathizing as we lead the caller to prayer. 


Here is an empathy statement with all the L.A.U.R.A components: 


“I am so sorry to hear about the tragic fire you had at home that led to the loss of so many precious valuables and memories.  I can only imagine what you must be feeling right now.  I haven’t personally experienced a fire in my home, but I have experienced the grief and fear that comes with a traumatic event, and I remember how difficult that was.  I am so thankful you called so we can pray about this for you today.  Let’s take this matter to the Lord in prayer…”

Navigate Needs 

Begin to navigate the needs of the caller through customer service initiatives and friendly language. 


Ways to Ensure that We Effectively Navigate Needs

1. Ownership - Remember, you are a member of a team that is focused on giving great experiences!  When the caller is talking to you, they are effectively talking to CBN! 

Ways to Control the Call Successfully
  • Informed Silence -  Check in with the caller every 30 seconds to keep them informed of your actions. 
  • Place the Caller on Hold -  Place the caller on hold when you need more than a minute to process the caller's request.
  • Apologize - When it becomes apparent that you have made a mistake, by the caller pointing it out or by your own realization, apologize for it. 
  • Model Statements of Ownership - Take responsibility by ensuring you meet the caller’s needs! 
  • Move with a Sense of Direction - Our destination is to provide compassionate prayer ministry and outstanding customer service. Assess whether you are taking the customer to that destination or detouring/derailing. 

2. Investigate and Confirm - Ask open-ended questions to encourage a caller to provide more information if their request that requires that we use our investigative skills to dig a little deeper.
3. Connect to the Vine -  (John 15:1-8)

Inspire with Service

If the caller has additional needs that need to be met, along with any additional prayer requests, take the time to continue providing excellent service by modeling ownership, continued empathy, and continued gratitude.


Ways to Ensure we Effectively Inspire with Exceptional Service

1. Remember the Golden Rule - Treat others the way you want to be treated!  Ask yourself, “Is this the kind of service I would want?”, “Is this how I would want to be treated?” 
2. Remember Our Why - We are here to be the love of Jesus to every caller!  Share the love of Jesus by expressing the Fruit of the Spirit.
3. Always Be Solution-Driven - Go above and beyond to make the caller’s experience great by finding and offering them solutions!

Click on the accordions below to access calling processing how-to's for various agent activities. Each accordion highlights a type of agent activity that may take place while on a call. 

Processing Prayer

  1. Under the Category List select Prayer
  2. Proceed to ask the caller their prayer request. "I would be happy to pray with you. What can we pray about?"
  3. Empathize with what the caller has shared
    • Be mindful to write down in the Prayer Request section what they said so that you can remember the request.
  4. After you have assessed the needs of the caller proceed to select the Prayer Type to access recommended scripture on the subject.
  5. Select "Save & Continue" and proceed to pray with the caller
  6. After completion of the prayer, share faith statement to encourage the caller

Processing Giving

  1. Under the Category List select Giving
  2. Select the appropriate project the caller has asked to give under the Project List
  3.  Proceed to ask the caller "How may I assist you in your giving today"
  4. Choose the appropriate response under Caller Response based on the caller's answer

    • For Ex. Unsure, doesn't state amount, Less than $20/month, Between $20- $39/month, One-time gift

  5. After choosing the Caller Response, read the script attached to the caller response on the left

  6. Choose the following option that applies, ("Make a Pledge", "Make a Pledge + AGENT INITIATED INCREASE DVD", or "Inquiry Only")

Call Processing Tips

Click on the accordions below to access calling processing tips for various agent activities. Each accordion highlights a type of activity that may take place while on a call. 

Address/Email Verification

Why We Verify

When we have accurate caller information, we are able to strengthen the caller’s trust and give them a great experience. For security reasons, we ask our callers to provide their information, to prevent fraud, or to prevent us from misidentifying the caller. Accurately verifying information also helps us to keep our records accurate. 
Delete

How To Verify

When there is an existing account (non 9999+), we ask the caller to verify their information as follows: 

“For the protection of your account, could you please verify your full name, address and email?” 

Note: If the caller information does not match, you may use the Account Lookup tool or the Account Information dropdown (pictured below), to locate the correct account.


Things to Remember:

  • Always ask the caller to verify their information. Do not read contact information to the caller. 
  • Always ask the caller for their name instead of reading it from the screen. Even though in most cases, there is a name and account number displayed in Scripter, you can’t be sure that the person you are speaking with is the person whose name is on the account.         
  • Be sure to ask the caller, “May I have your name please?” Do not say phrases like, “you are” or “what’s your name”    


If the caller provides a first and last name, be sure to address the caller by Mr./Mrs./Ms. Last Name (unless the caller asks you to do otherwise)

  •  Pledges

Membership Ask

Why We Ask

Partnership with CBN gives an opportunity to show concern in a tangible way. Many people may have a desire to give, but no outlet by which to do so. When we invite a person to partner with the ministry, we are giving them the opportunity to sow a seed into good ground.

CBN blesses people in so many ways. We provide assistance to people devastated by natural disaster, we share the Gospel with those who have never heard, we feed hungry children and provide medical care in desperate situations. 

The fact is, without our partners, we would not be able to do all that we are doing in sharing the love of God and helping the poor and disadvantaged. That is why it is important that we give people the opportunity to give. This invitation is what we call the ''ask''.

When To Ask

By accepting the Membership Ask, callers are given the opportunity to invest into CBN ministries. It is important that we do not extend the Membership Ask on every call. 

Scripter is programmed to choose the best script to use for each call based on a caller's recent call history to prevent asking for donations too soon or frequently. 

The "Sensitive Areas"

It is important that we extend the Membership invitation even in situation that may appear sensitive in nature. 

When callers allude to the following, it makes us ponder whether to extend the Membership invitation.


  • Lost their job or is currently out of work
  • Serious health issues
  • Living with others or in an assisted living community
  • Disabled or has a chronic ailment
  • Financial need such as needing funds for a vehicle, rent or mortgage, college tuition, home repairs, etc.

In these instances, always be sensitive and empathetic as you extend the Membership Ask. We always want the Lord to lead as we give the caller the opportunity to respond, "Yes" or "No."

When Not To Ask
(Inappropriate to Ask)

There are situations in which it is inappropriate to ask for membership:

  • Those who are institutionalized, such as residents of a hospital, inmates in jail/prison, and residents in a rehab center
  • People who are homeless living on the street
  • Those going through immediate grief because a loved one or family member has recently passed away. The grief is happening now!
  • Those who have experienced a catastrophic loss like a natural disaster (hurricane, tornado, earthquake, and flood), a home fire, a sudden home foreclosure or eviction
  • Urgent Needs such as suicide, abuse or threat calls, or immediate emergencies
  • Underage callers -- these are callers who have identified themselves as being under the age of 18

On these calls, it is more important to minister the love and peace of God rather than asking them to support the ministry financially. When you encounter these situations, select the "Bypass - Inappropriate to Ask'' option:

Membership Ask Options

Scripter has several call processing options available to select based on the caller's response to the Membership Ask question:

  • Yes - Accepts the Membership Ask
    • Proceed to process pledge or donation
  • No - Declines the Membership Ask
    • Proceed to close call
  • Maybe - Uncertain or responds with, ''Not now", or something similar
    • Proceed to close call
  • Does Not Want to Give By Phone - Caller expresses reluctance or reservations about giving over the phone 
    • Proceed to gather email of address of the caller: 

  • The caller will receive a personalized email (agent first name and job title), that will direct them to the CBN Giving page where they can securely make their donation:

  • Already a Member- Indicates they are a partner or monthly donor
    • Proceed to thank the caller for their membership and close call
  • Bypass - Situation is within the 'Inappropriate to Ask'' criteria
    • Proceed to close call
  • Caller Hung Up- Caller hangs up on the Membership Ask
    • Proceed to close call
  • Donated on a Previous Transaction- Caller already made a donation while on the call
    • Proceed to close call
