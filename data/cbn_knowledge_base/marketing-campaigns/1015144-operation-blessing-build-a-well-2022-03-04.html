Operation Blessing Build A Well

Written by Darnetta Carbon | Last published at: March 04, 2022

Description 

Operation Blessing’s Clean Water Project builds wells in regions around the world where the water supply is often contaminated or scarce and/or where there is the greatest need. 

Through the Build A Well project, Operation Blessing has built numerous wells and helped serve and save the lives of more than 7 million people.


Briefing Notes

Release Date: 3/4/22

Internal Process Change – Water Well Donations Temporarily Discontinued (T2)

In February, Operation Blessing mailed letters to Major Donors who contributed towards funding a well (hand-drilled well). Due to a continued struggle with COVID-19 government lockdowns, travel restrictions, and other pandemic-related impacts on our staff and work OB was unable to build as many wells as anticipated last year and it may take another 24 months before well installation. 

If partners no longer wish to wait for the well to be built, they can (1) redistribute their gift in one of Operation Blessing’s larger community water projects, (2) another area of ministry, or (3) receive a full refund.  

If callers want to distribute their funds to another area of the ministry or receive a refund, please process these calls as follows:  

Category: Account Services 

Project: Pledge Problems/Changes 

Agent Note: Select Operation Blessing Pledge Problems/Changes and submit the referral documenting the partner’s request to redistribute or refund their funds.

Well Sponsorship

How To Sponsor a Well 

  • Full - one time gift of $4000 (or more)
  • Co - one time gift of $2000
  • Well Project - Monthly gift of $200 for 20 months (This will add up to the cost of one well)


  • If a caller is unable to give the one-time gift, offer the monthly option.
  • If a caller is unable to give on a monthly basis, any donation amount will be greatly appreciated and help to provide water to those who are in need. 
    • Donations will be combined with other gifts and we will build a well in the area where there is the greatest need.

What Do I Receive When I Sponsor a Well?

Information Packet

When you sponsor a well, within 7-14 business days, you will receive a confirmation letter of your donation along with an information packet which includes the following contents:

  • A picture of the water project, matted and suitable for framing
  • An information sheet that tells about the village and the dedication service  (you can visit your well and, in most cases, you can also be present at the well dedication)
  • A small map showing where the village is located   
  • A collage of photographs of the villagers who are served by the clean water 
    • It takes approximately 9-12 months to complete a project. During that time, we hold a dedication ceremony, gather reports and photographs to prepare your packet

Can I Pick the Country for a Well?

All OB wells will be assigned to countries where OB staff has identified as most in need, unless the donor requests a specific country. 

Please do not promise that a well can be drilled in a particular country. Please send a call referral to determine if the requested country can be accommodated.


We are unable to drill wells in the U.S. or in countries where we're not currently working. 

Delete

How Are You Able to Keep the Costs Low?

We are able to keep our costs low because of several important factors:

  • We save the cost of training and sending U.S. employees because all of our wells are drilled by local workers and supervised by our in-country staff.
  • We buy our pumps (and other materials in bulk) from local vendors whenever possible. This ensures we have the lowest cost possible for all of our supplies.
  • We tailor our program to meet the need for clean water effectively and efficiently.
