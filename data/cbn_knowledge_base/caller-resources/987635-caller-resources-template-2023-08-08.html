How to Recognize an Active Suicide Call

How do you know if a caller is experiencing a suicide situation? These four scenarios are the suicide call indicators:

  • Having Suicidal Thoughts - The caller says they are having suicidal thoughts.
  • Has the Means and a Plan - The caller states they have the means and a plan to commit suicide.Â 
  • Imminent Threat of Suicide - The caller is making threatening statements that indicate immediate harm to themselves or others.Â 
  • Suicide in Progress - The caller states that they have taken steps to commit suicide. Â 

Bottom Line: Do not assume or guess. They must say the words!Â

How to Recognize an Abuse Call

How do you know that a caller is experiencing an abuse situation?

  • Caller mentions Current / Recent AbuseÂ 
    • Anything that has happened in the last 24 hours
    • Caller states they have been abused
  • Threat of Abuse Made
    • The caller states that a threat has been made against them and/or they are fearful of being harmed
  • Child AbuseÂ 
    • Caller suspects active child abuse - physical or sexual
    • Caller reports a threat of abuse towards their childÂ 
      • CBN is a MUST REPORT organization, meaning that if we hear a report that a child has been abused, or there is suspicion of abuse, we must notify authorities. There is no time-limit on when the abuse occurred.
  • Imminent Threat of AbuseÂ 
    • The caller indicates there is an imminent threat of physical or sexual abuse
