Briefing Notes

Release Date: 7/7/23

Process Refresher – Disaster Relief Request for Assistance Project (T2) 

As a reminder, please remember that the ONLY instance in which to use the “Current Disaster Related Assistance Request” form is when a BN announces that the Operation Blessing Team has been deployed to a specific area and is receiving requests for assistance. 

Any other call requesting assistance should be processed by using the “Non-Disaster Related Assistance Statement” that will provide other resources to the caller. Although these situations are devastating and your caller genuinely needs help, we unfortunately are not able to offer individual assistance. 

Therefore, when you fill out the Disaster Related Request for Assistance form, it gives callers false hope, and puts the burden on OB to call each person back, stating that we are not able to fulfill their request.

Some example scenarios to use the Non-Disaster Related Assistance Statement are as follows:

  • Requests for help to pay utility or medical bills
  • Requests help building wheelchair accessibility ramps
  • Requests help clearing debris from property

Please process these calls as follows:

Category: Comment

Project: OB Disaster Relief Request for Assistance

Agent Note: OB does not deploy teams to every disaster area. If the caller is requesting assistance in an area where OB is not deployed, or has already left the area, please apologize and let them know we are not active in the area. Then, provide resources from the Non-Disaster Related Statement.

Disaster-Related Request For Assistance Resources

Through the generosity of our partners, Operation Blessing strives to meet the needs of communities impacted by natural disasters.

If you receive a call from someone in need of assistance from a disaster that OB is deployed to, submit a Disaster Relief Request for Assistance Form. They will be contacted by an OB representative within 24-48 hours.

Please ONLY submit this Disaster Relief Request form in response to a disaster OB is currently deployed to.

Request Form

The form asks the following questions:

  • Address and Contact Information of the Impacted Area/Individual
  • Brief Description of Damage
  • The Type of Work Needed:
    • Tarp Work: Using tarps to secure and protect areas of a home from further damage or leaking, such as roof damage
    • Debris Removal
    • Muck & Gut: A type of work in which items (such as clothing, furniture, and appliances) are removed from a water-damaged building, as well as the removal of the damaged drywall 
    • Packaging and Salvaging
    • Chainsaw
  • Additional Special Needs
  • Whether the individual has the following:
    • Applied for Federal Disaster Assistance
    • Home Insurance
    • Flood Insurance

Disaster Relief Requests for Assistance

Please do not submit the Disaster Relief Requests for Assistance unless you see a "Yes" in the table below for a specific disaster. 

There will also be a Briefing Note informing you when OB is receiving these Requests for Assistance, as opposed to when they are no longer accepting them.

If the disaster is not listed at all, the answer is no, OB is not receiving Relief Requests for Assistance, in which case you may use the Non-Disaster statement that enables you to gently direct the caller to connect with local sources for help.
